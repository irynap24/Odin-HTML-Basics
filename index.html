Facts about Orchids

Orchids are one of the largest and most diverse plant families, with over 25,000 species worldwide and more than 100,000. They grow in a variety of environments, from tropical rainforests to arid deserts. hybrids.

Orchids rely on a unique relationship with fungi. Their seeds are tiny and lack the necessary nutrients to grow on their own, so they depend on fungi to provide them with essential nutrients during germination.

Orchids have some of the longest-lasting flowers in the plant kingdom. Their blooms can last anywhere from weeks to several months, depending on the species, making them a popular choice for decorative houseplants.

Many orchids have evolved highly specialized methods to attract specific pollinators, like bees, moths, and even hummingbirds. Some even mimic the appearance or scent of certain insects to lure them in for pollination.
